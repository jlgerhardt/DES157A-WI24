2023 Guesthouse, LLC

First Winter, 2023

Medium

Graphite, marker, and oil on canvas.

Dimensions

30 x 16 in. (70.2 x 40.6 cm)

Background

This was the last time I had time to create anything physical for pure personal expression. The composition includes a variety of different subjects that were relevant to me at the time. In the background a metal logo for my friend JITA is the largest subject. A green top representing my final project for DES 051 can be seen, with the remnants of a sketch depicting a random hallway from the SF MOMA placed over top.

Return

waves, 2022

Medium

Graphite and oil on canvas.

Dimensions

36 x 36 in. (91.4 x 91.4 cm)

Background

The largest canvas I have ever used. Originally, the painting was for an album cover for a music project called waves//: demos that a few of my close friends had been working on. I was not able to complete the painting before they wanted to release the demo. That group would become GUESTHOUSE (shameless plug)

I had planned to create an entire landscape to accompany the gradient streaks but ultimately shelved the project to pursue others.

Return

Book From The Sky, 2022

Medium

Graphite and oil on canvas.

Dimensions

36 x 36 in. (91.4 x 91.4 cm)

Background

A combination of my favorite elements for this medium; typography and surrealism.

The characters are meaningless- they are completely fictional and hold zero value. Inspired by the chinese artist Xu Bing's installation Book from the Sky. The characters in my piece are from the first few pages of the installation.

Turrel, 2022

Medium

Oil on canvas.

Dimensions

20 x 20 in. (50.8 x 50.8 cm)

Background

The first piece I experimented with smooth linear gradients. My interest in surrealism is especially present here.

Named after the artist James Turrel, who uses light to create beautiful gradient patterns. A heavy inspiration for the linear gradient motif.

No Longer Human, 2022

Medium

Marker and oil on canvas.

Dimensions

18 x 24 in. (45.7 x 60.9 cm)

Background

The text is a passage from a book called No Longer Human by Osamu Dazai.

The large text on the right reads 'ACTA N', an incomplete latin phrase 'ACTA NON VERBA', which translates to 'actions, not words'.

I wanted to connect the literature I was consuming at the time with the medium.
